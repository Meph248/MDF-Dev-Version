Plump Helmet Men

 

- PHM can have between 1-10 children at a time, averaging 5 per female.  If you're not careful, they may attain the majority so fast that you won't be able to bring down their numbers quickly enough.


- PHM will do a headcount of all non-PHM creatures in the immediate area.  If they realize that they are the majority, they will rebel.  It takes them about 1-2 weeks to figure out that they are the majority.  This has no visible effect at first.


- A rebellious phase lasts for 1 week.


- Rebelling PHM will be intimidated by nearby creatures.  They will usually not attack unless they outnumber their opponents.


- Rebelling PHM will not be intimidated by unconscious or sleeping creatures.


- Rebelling PHM can sometimes drain blood from sleeping creatures like vampires.  This effect lasts for half a year after the rebellion actually starts.


- PHM cannot tell one species from another, but they can tell if the creature had been in a fight within the last year and did not run away.  Creatures who fight can intimidate up to 5 PHM at once, so they will usually not be attacked unless they are greatly outnumbered.


- Once a group of PHM decide to attack, they do so by going berserk.  Nearby creatures may be attacked even if they were not the actual target.


- Sometimes rebelling PHM will attack each other.


- Rebellious PHM living for a long time underground have a small chance of inflicting necrosis on themselves.  This will generate miasma, causing bad thoughts.


- PHM can pick locks - forbidding doors will not control them.


- Rebellious PHM cannot be contained reliably by a far-off pasture, because they have a small chance of randomly going berserk.


- Trying to contain PHM behind walls to prevent gang attacks is a risky strategy - if they don't see any non-PHM creature for a while, they will rebel entirely and may rush the door the moment it is opened.


- Soldiers will not obey direct orders to kill a rebellious PHM that is now docile.  However, they may choose to attack one on their own if it had been seen killing someone (the ones with names are the ones that made a kill).


- Killing rebellious PHM has a chance of starting a loyalty cascade.


- The best way of controlling PHM with limited dwarfpower is to pasture them nearby a major pathway, making sure that they can see all the dwarves that pass by.  Large numbers of tame animals can also prevent PHM from rebelling.  Separating them into small groups can help prevent attacks during a rebellion, as can the presence of guards that have been in combat.  Or you could go the simplest method: just eat them!

General Info

Skills: New skills like runesmithing, writing or praying, are included in the mod. None of them are important at the start, since they are all used for the new workshops. The most important skills are still mason, carpenter, farmer and smiths.

Combat: Only military caste dwarves can enter martial trances, but otherwise nothing has changed. If you want melee, ranged or traps, thats still your choice to make.

Weapons: In addition to all vanilla weapons, you can also make throwing weapons. These are good for melee and ranged. You can make powerful great-weapon, only wielded with two hands. A javelin thrower makes a better crossbow, and flint-lock pistols and muskets are the highest achievement of ranged weaponry.

All weapons can be improved in the weaponry, rune-coated and poisoned.

Ammo: Besides vanilla ammo, you can now make broadhead, hammerhead and piercing ammo. These work like slashing damage, blunt damage and piercing damage. Burning or exploding ammo can be made at the chemist.

Armor: Besides vanilla armor, you have access to padded leather armor. This can be worn underneath your metal armor. Any metal armor can also be upgraded to platearmor in the armory and rune-coated at the runesmith.

Materials: In addition to vanilla materials you have access to: refined wood, a difference between tanned skin, scale, chitin and shell, an upgrade system for these animal materials, higher metals like titanium, wolfram, cobalt or chrome, higher alloys like mithril or volcanic. The best is still the delicious blue candy deep underground.

Pets: Many normal human domestic animals have been removed and replaced with more dwarven alternatives. You can have drakes, whose scales make fine armor, mastiffs and shaggy badgerdogs, rock-crabs, leatherwing-bats that can be shorn for leather... special pets include the Ward of Armok, which protects your fort against possessions (read the manual), constructs like golems, landmines and turrets.

Food: There are some new food, including roasted seeds, cooked eggs, bread, candy, cave-fungus cheese and diggable living-rock, which can be butchered. You can also make sausages from meat and vermin remains. Dont ask.

Drink: Alcohol is just as important as ever. But now you can select which drinks to make, have a bigger selection and even two more buildings for it. The brewery has a slightly higher outcome and can make fuel from alcohol, and the Alchemist can brew special plants into potions, drink with magical abilities.

Digging: Mining has not been changed. But some shallow rock types and rubble patches only leave dust when mined, which forces you to dig deeper for real rock. Fossils, relics and treasure can be dug up from ruins, your archeologist can identify and restore them. You can also stumble upon the Gems of Armok, which have special effects tied to them, like blessing the miner, or water, magma and metal generation.

Embark: Your embark selection is pretty much the same as in vanilla. You dont necessarily need an anvil, because you can make them from ore in the smelter, but otherwise everything is the same. Some more pets, some more metals, but nothing that stands out.

Nobles: All vanilla nobles are still there. You also have a psychiatrist who calms down tantruming dwarves and an overseer, which is used in succession forts to mark the current player.

Magic: There is an advanced, very complicated magic system in place. I wont touch the subject here, see the building section for the Wizards School and the Altars of Magic.

Tech: The more advanced buildings have a multitude of sources. Some require magma, others blueprints or special items traded or stolen from other civs. The highest tech level is reached from end-game civs. Trade with gnomes, kill frostgiants or automatons for reagents.

Caste System: Dwarves are members of guilds. Most migrants are just peasants, but you can build a guildhall and join up. Guildmembers learn associated skills twice as fast. The same is true for soldiers. Build a garrison and join a military group, doubling the fitting skill learn rates. You have aggresive legionnaires, protective guards, sniping marksdwarves and fast and agile wrestlers.

Important stuff: Besides 'Temperature: On', I honestly cant think of anything that needs to play. Only the metallurgist comes to mind, because all alloy-making reactions have been moved there. Otherwise all vanilla DF rules apply, but there is nothing that MasterworkDF forces you to do to survive. If you play with 'secret fun', you might have some unpleasant visitors in your fort that need the help of the temple/ward of armok to clear out, and 'diseases' needs an apothecary to combat. These are all optional of course...

Just keep in mind that vanilla DF has one invader race, Goblins. MasterworkDF has sixteen. This means up a lot more !FUN!,snatcher, thieves, sieges and ambushes. Again, these are optional. You can even add another fifteenfortress defence races to it.

WORKSHOPS

  • Apothecary's Study: Makes hospital supplies and will be used for a future disease-centered update.
  • Archeologist's Study: Works with fossils, relics and treasure to make good, but random, items.
  • Researcher's Study: Research the new buildings, otherwise you cant build most of them.
  • Alchemist's Chambers: Makes magic potions from special plants and can transmute metals. Works with special gems for water, magma or metal production.
  • Chemistry Laboratory: Can remove rust from iron and steel, and make explosives.
  • Embassy: Invite caravans, diplomats and migrants, or start sieges yourself. Great for islands.
  • Military Garrison: Join one of four military groups for combat boni.
  • Guildhall: Join one of twelve guilds for skill boni.
  • Tavern: Play music to buff nearby dwarves, gamble for coins or start a brawl to train misc combat skills.
  • Trade Storehouse: Unpack special tradegoods, crates that contain a multitude of items.
  • Toxicologist: Make poisons and apply them to weapons, trapcomponents or ammo.
  • Bonecutter: Make many items and furniture from bones.
  • Brewery: Make specific drinks, water or fuel from alcohol.
  • Fishpond: Indoor fish farming. Great for desert maps.
  • Stables: Feed grazers even indoors using plants or hay.
  • Slaugherhouse: Butchers shop for megabeasts. Gives a trophy, a rewards and the beasts soul.
  • Sawmill: Cut wooden logs into four planks, or make wood from mined gnarled roots.
  • Wood Splitting Block: Cut rough wood into smooth wood or make planks for constructions.
  • Wheight Bench: Train attributes like strength, endurance or toughness.
  • Thatchery: Make furniture from plants. Very light, and re-growable. Great for bins.
  • Warbeast Kennels: Train or armor your warbeasts in iron or steel to make them a lot more deadly.
  • Furniture Workshop: Creates sets of furniture from wood, bone, metal or stone. Eases fortress management.
  • Gemcutter's Workshop: Make many items from gems, can also split gemcrafts and large gems into three small ones.
  • Stonecutter's Workshop: Make many items from rock, even beds, bins and barrels. Or anvils.
  • Tailor's Workshop: Unravels cloth back into thread and can make clothing sets.
  • Trap Engineer's Workshop: Upgrade your trap components to give them more power.
  • Gemforge: Make armors, weapons and ammo from gems.
  • Rockforge: Make armors, weapons and ammo from rock.
  • Dungeon Deco: Adds decorational rugs, candelabras, braziers and totems.
  • Potted Plants: Adds decorational potted plants, potted trees and patches of grass.
  • Fountain: Adds a decorational fountain. Also trains swimming.
  • Scriptorium: Makes ink, books and notebooks for the libraries. Can also Quicksave the game.
  • Library of Warfare: Trains combat skills quickly from books.
  • Library of Mind and Body: Trains medical and trading skills quickly from books.
  • Library of Farmwork: Trains farming skills quickly from books.
  • Library of Crafting: Trains crafting skills quickly from books.
  • Library of Philosophy: Trains social skills quickly from books. Ha, as if.
  • Shrine of Armok: Pray to recieve a single object. Slow return rate, but good for moods.
  • Altar of Armok: Offer objects or sacrifice a dwarf to Armok to recieve a special object.
  • Temple of Armok: Sacrifice dwarves for even greater rewards and protect yourself from hidden threats.
  • Magic Buildings: 1 magic school and 6 magical altars. Too complex to be summed up here.
  • Colosseum: Spawn different creatures to fight and earn a reward from metal colossi. Good for training. Needs sprites to be pastured on the workshop.
  • Water/Magma Well: Spawns magma. Can be used for magma landmines.
  • (*Magma) Steam Engine: Needs water and fuel to produce power. Power your machines indoors.


FURNACES

  • Brazier, Candelabra, Fireplace: Starts fires. Can also do a 9 month long fire, great for cleanups.
  • Brick Oven: Makes blocks of any color. Designed for megaprojects and fortress color coding.
  • Crematory: Creates ash and fuel from any burnable item. Again, good for cleanups.
  • Finishing Forge: Hammers native ore into metals, does not need fuel. Has a low efficiency.
  • Slagpit: Metalworking produces waste, called slag. Get rid of it here, or make concrete or fertilizer with it.
  • (*Magma) Metallurgist: Produces all alloys. Simple.
  • (*Magma) Blast Furnace: Smelts batches of ore. Works like a very big, fast smelter.
  • (*Magma) Crucible: Smelts new and advanced metals. Also makes bronze and steel alloys more efficiently
  • (*Magma) Armory: Upgrades armor to plate armor.
  • (*Magma) Glassforge: Makes many glass items that the glassfurnace is missing.
  • (*Magma) Gunsmith: Creates muskets and flint-lock pistols. Needs blackpowder from the chemist to work.
  • (*Magma) Heavy Siegeworks: Cheaper ballista bolts and siege engine parts. Why use only wood?
  • (*Magma) Rune Armory: Applies runes to armors and coats. Gives special abilities, 15 to choose from. Multiple runes on one item possible.
  • (*Magma) Rune Weaponry: Applies runtes to weapons, ammo and traps. Gives special abilitis, 15 to choose from. Multiple runes on one item possible.
  • (*Magma) Weaponry: Upgrades any weapon to a superior or legendary model. Destroys any runes or poison attached to it.
  • Machine Factory: Construct turrets and landmines. 8 different types for each. Needs toolkits to be pastured on the workshop.
  • Volcanic Golem Forge: Transform a dwarf into a massive metal warrior. Sword, Spear or Hammergolem available. Lose a dwarf, win a statue of war.
  • Volcanic Foundry: Meld obsidian, silver, steel into volcanic metal, the best metal you can produce yourself. Does extra damage to frost giants.
  • Warpstone Pool: Create your own FBs.
