Category Titles

All workshops are separated into different categories in the build menu. These category titles can also be build, they will spell out their name. This can be used to label areas in-game, which is great for succession forts. They cost no build materials.

A big amount of new reactions can be done here. You can extract new kinds of metal strands from metals bars, enabling silver, golden, mithril or orichalcum clothing. You can craft single instruments or crafts from boulders, random instruments or toys from boulders, make rope from leather, make candles from tallow or wax. Illumination, like braziers, candelabras and fireplaces can be created from rock boulders.

The crafts section for bone and shell contain all armor items you can usually produce. The wooden and rock crafts now include hilts, which are needed for weapon production, as well as smoking pipes.

Dye, Blood, Graphite and Ash can be made into ink. You require 3 pots to fill when doing this. Quills can be produced from feathers. Ink and quills are used in the Scriptorium.

With enough feathers and a bed, you can make a feathered mattress on a bed, a prized trade-good and high-value item for your dwarves.

Use wicker or hay and a shirt to make Scarecrows. Scarecrows can't fight or move, but are good to reveal ambushes. Pasture them around your fort for early detection and to scare away curious animals.

Many new sorts of food are available. Roasting inedible seeds make them edible, boiling eggs does the same to raw eggs. Mined "cave fungus" can be made into cheese. Another new cheese can be made from boozebelly goats, which is alcoholic and can be fermented into drinks.

You can use meat to make ground meat or, combined with vermin remains, sausages. You can also extract blood from fresh meat, for all kinds of purposes from sacrifices to dark magic.

You can use flour or sugar milled from plants to either bake bread or make candy. Note that DF can't find ingredients inside bags that are inside barrels. So store this in a stockpile without barrels.

If you farm silk moths you can boil their cocoons here to extract the silk threads.

You can boil horn/hoof materials here to create glue. Glue is needed for binding books and the making of plywood, and other sources include spore-trees or bloated tubers.

TANNING: Adds specific tanning reactions for each animal skin type. You can tan skin, tough skin, chitin, shell or scales, into an amount of leather based on the creatures size. Usually around 2-4 pieces, smaller creatures might give none, while massive monsters give dozens.

LEATHER: Normal leather can be used for suede(3) or vellum(3) from one piece. Suede is very thin leather, useless for combat, but good for backpacks or quivers. Vellum is used for the library system. Alternatives are papyrus (thatchery) or paper (screw press).

FUR/STOMACH/GUT/HORN: The newest additions are the tanning of a stomach directly into a waterskin, the making of rope from guts/intestines and tanning a pelt into a fur. A fur is the very same as leather, but also protects from cold. Fur is needed for glacier embarks. If you don't want the fur, you can shave of the hairs to create a piece of leather and a unit of hair thread. Horns can be shaped into a small dagger, for earliest militias.

FEATHERS: Birds leave feathers upon being butchered, which can be used to make quills for writing, or feather matresses for beds.

UPGRADES: You can upgrade leather by boiling it in oil to harden it, then add bonestuds to the hardened leather to make studded leather, and then stitch several studded leather pieces together to create lamellar leather. Each type will be a little tougher, with lamellar being the best. The oil is preserved to 85%, and studs can be made from bone, vermin or horn/hoof. The same upgrade system in a smaller form applies to shell, scale and chitin. Using 2 units of it you can combine it to one unit of the "plate" version.

Material strength:

Leather = Vanilla Leather. ::: Tough leather = Copper.

Studded leather = Bronze. ::: Lamellar leather = Iron.

Chitin = Bronze. ::: Chitinplate = Iron, very light.

Scale = Bronze. ::: Scalepate = Iron, fireproof.

Shell = Bronze. ::: Shellplate = Between iron and steel, heavy.

You can create bows, upgrade all ranged weapons and make specialized ammo from wood or bone. The Fletcher's Workshop is the advancement of the bowyer.

Bows require wood and thread to be made. You have two kinds of upgrades available for ranged weapons, you can either bayonett them using a large dagger, or add 2 mechanisms to them to increase their rate of fire. You can even do both if you wish, creating a bladed repeating weapon.

The ammo comes in three types, broadhead (cutting damage), hammerhead (blunt damage) and piercing (piercing damage). In essence they work the same as sword, hammer and spear. You can make bolts, arrows and javelins from wood and bone. You can find the metal counterparts of this ammo in the Ammo Caster among the furnaces.

The Sawmill is the upgrade to the Wood Splitting Block. It has the same reactions, cutting rough wood and roots, making planks and cleaning sawdust. In addition to that you can make plywood from sawdust and glue.

The most important feature is tree farming. Each kind of tree in the mod can be planted in farm plots, if you are in the correct biome and have the acorns or spores. Trees, Ironbark, Steeloak, Fungiwood, Sporetrees, Crystal Wood, Glassthorns, Nethercaps, Shadethorn, Feather and Glumprong can be farmed. Seeds must be brought at embark, bought from caravans, or discovered from cutting collected tree saplings. The Herbalist's Lab might also provide access to seeds.

All trees grow all year and need at least one year to mature. The tree saplings are treated as plants and stored as such. Only the Sawmill can cut them into wood logs.

The Sawmill can also break down any wooden item you made into wooden logs and sawdust.

Every megabeast will leave a special corpse in this mod and can be butchered in the slaughterhouse to receive 3 items. A trophy that can be kept in a display case or traded away, a special reward that is specific to the creature, and a megabeast soul. The soul can be used in the Megabeast Kennels to train a single dwarf instantly to legendary in a chosen combat skill, or to revive the beast. Keep in mind that titans and forgotten beasts are not megabeasts and can't be affected by modding.

Megabeasts also give 60 meat, or 50 boulders, or 25 metal bars upon being butchered, depending on the bodytype of the beast. You will always get 1 heart.

Example: Kill dragon, butcher dragon corpse, get 10 straps of dragonscale. A steel quality, fireproof leather type. You will also get the dragon skull, worth a lot, and the soul.

Guilds are dwarven castes with higher learn rates then normal dwarves. They learn all skills related to their guild twice as fast. There are 12 guilds in total, you can find more info on them in the "general changes" section.

Joining a guild costs 500 gold. The dwarf will become a guild apprentice and stay inside the workshop for one week, before transforming into a full guild member. Dwarves can also switch guilds or leave them.

Miners of the masons guild are more resistant to warpstone and coaldust.

Important: Guilds, Military Groups, Mages and Priests are mutually exclusive. You can't have a masons-guild legionnaire Airmage Apostle. Priests can no longer join any guilds.

The garrison is the military counterpart to the guilds. Military groups are dwarven castes with higher learn rates then normal dwarves. They learn all skills related to their group twice as fast. There are 4 groups in total, you can find more info on them in the "general changes" section.

Joining a group costs 2500 gold and 1 week of time. The dwarf will become a recruit in that time and stay inside the garrison, then transform into a military dwarf. Dwarves can also switch groups or leave them.

The four groups are very distinctive, offensive (Legion), defensive (Guard), ranged (Marksman) and speed (Wrestlers) They are also the only dwarves that can enter martial trances and that get positive thoughts from fighting.

Important: Guilds, Military Groups, Mages and Priests are mutually exclusive. You can't have a masons-guild legionnaire Airmage Apostle.

You can also sound the alarm: It can wake sleeping dwarves and cancels all breaks and parties. This allows your army to be ready for an attack. The reaction is free and can be run as often as you want, but mind that the loud noise and lack of sleep will cause bad thoughts.
