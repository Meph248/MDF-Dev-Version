Getting started

Installation

This mod is pre-installed. Just download, unpack, that's it. When you are reading this, you already installed the mod, congratulations. Please do not copy it over vanilla DF or older mod versions. Just unpack it into a new folder.

To alter the mod, you can run the Masterwork Dwarf Fortress.exe. You will need to do this to set Succubus as a playable race. Just open it up, click on 'Civilizations' and you will see a large list of civilizations. Currently Dwarves, Orcs, Kobolds, Gnomes, Warlocks, Succubi and Humans are potentially playable. Tick the box under 'Fort Mode' and all is done.

If you just want to play one race, it is recommended to turn the other races non-playable. It will make embarking easier, since you don't have to choose your race ingame.

A typical succubus site

The succubi use a mix of surface and underground features. They enjoy expansive palaces where dark stone and gem windows meet decorated furniture.  Each fort in their kingdom must be treated as a work of art.

But the finely decorated bedrooms hide a darker secret. In the underground, dark temples maintain a link between the surface and the horrors below, enabling the succubi to summon powerful monsters while the witches sacrifice the souls of their enemies for their own leisure, or their enemies' pain.

If possible, succubi prefer to embark on evil biomes. They particulary enjoy drinking wine made from evil plants and their cattle does not suffer there. Beware of the undead who will never understand desires the way lust demons do.

The embark preparation

You will be able to live with a standard inventory. However you have to keep in mind that sieges will start very early. So it is a good idea to prepare one of your original succubi as a trained fighter.

Skills

  • Same as dwarves for 6 succubi: miners, lumberjack, carpenter, mason, farmer
  • Ideally one combat capable succubus to appoint as lady of pain.
  • Do not separate organizer and bookkeeper. The keeper of secrets fill both roles.

Items

  • Standard items a regular fort uses. What worked for dwarves will work for the succubi.
  • Your succubi are not alcohol dependant so drinks are not as vital if there is water.
  • You can buy surface plants at the embark screen.
  • A weapon and an helmet to equip your lady of pain. Bronze is the best budget metal.

Pets

  • Tentacle monsters are expensive but will provides an oil substitute, sets contaminant traps and are good fighters.
  • Orthus are similar to dogs but stronger, they can serve as bodyguards.
  • Nahashes or rat things will hunt vermins around.
  • You might start with 1 or 2 cauchemars. Those can follow in simple fights or provide your with early milk.

First things on the map

First appoint your lady of pain and create a squad for her. Get her to train quickly and give her two sparring mates after the first migration waves. Be careful of her happiness however, you can dig a room for her and making early furniture with your carpenter.

Otherwise the standard procedure of securing your supplies and making the fort sustainable and defended apply. You must keep in mind that 'good' guys are on the way so a drawbridge and basic defences are a priority.

Afterwards you can slaughter your starting pack animals. Turn their skulls into totems and you will be able to build a summoning circle to start bringing extra beasts. Their souls can be used at the temple of sin to offer a couple of extra moves to your warriors.

Advancing

Build a jeweler workshop and start cutting gems. Those will let you conserve souls at the Soul Well. Large gems also allow you to summon fire imps.

The temple of fire can add fire immunity to your warriors, combining that with fire imps, torrid kiss or other fire interactions will give you an edge.  Beware of friendly fire!

You can also make a worker fire immune and start digging space for a magma reservoir with a magma well designed to pour into it. Use your fire immune succubi to fill the reservoir. Building your furnaces on top of those will unlock most of the industry.

As an alternative to immigrants, making a large quantity of cage traps will allow you to turn prisoners into more succubi. Build their cage near a den of iniquity and, afterwards, free them using a lever. Be careful that their mount will remain hostile no matter what.

You can seek better armor materials, summoned basilisks can be sheared for horns. Turning their horns into bars in the smelter will yield a metal as strong as steel but much lighter. Basiliskine is great for armor making.

Otherwise you can make bloodsteel out of bodyparts, blood is produced at the kitchen.
